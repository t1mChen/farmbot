HarvestX : Week 9 presentation to client

Created by Xinxin Guo, last modified on Sep 22, 2023

Intro

We understand that helping potential customers truly understand the power and potential of FarmBot is not an easy task. It's in the FarmBot demo where they get their first exposure to this groundbreaking technology and learn how it is transforming agriculture. However, based on our observations, we have realized that the current demo does not fully showcase all of FarmBot's capabilities. This can lead to potential customers not fully grasping FarmBot's advantages and, consequently, reducing their desire to make a purchase.

Therefore, we have made the decision to unlock certain features within the demo to make it more realistic and feature-rich. As potential users are more likely to purchase products they understand and trust.

Photo

We believe that the coolest feature of Farmbot is the ability to remotely monitor the condition of your plants, allowing you to witness the process of their growth continuously. When using the original demo's photo section, we discovered that this feature is not supported in the demo version. Actually, it was only after watching Farmbot's YouTube tutorials that we learned that you could see photos of the plants on the map. Many potential customers will not spend time browsing Farmbot's tutorials before making a purchase, like we did. Perhaps this super-cool feature could be a decisive factor in their buying decision.

Movement

When potential users interact with the FarmBot demo, their primary desire is to get hands-on and witness the capabilities of FarmBot up close. They want to experience firsthand the thrill of operating FarmBot and manipulating the robotic arm. This kind of gamified experience can greatly enhance their interest and understanding of FarmBot's potential. Hence, we have decided to unlock the functionality that allows users to control and move the robotic arm during the demo, all while capturing photos in the process. This change is not just about adding a new feature; it's about providing potential users with an authentic FarmBot experience, allowing them to genuinely appreciate the convenience and innovation that FarmBot offers.

Tour & Shopping

We are planning to introduce operational guides for FarmBot during the next sprint. These guides will serve as valuable tools to help potential users navigate and operate FarmBot more easily. By providing step-by-step instructions and tips, we can ensure that the demo experience is smoother, enabling users to grasp FarmBot's potential and how it can be integrated into their own agricultural operations more quickly. But that's not all. We also have plans to add links to advertising materials and the purchase interface within the demo. These links will offer a direct pathway for potential users to explore more about FarmBot product and, if they are ready, to make a purchase. This streamlined approach ensures that interested users have easy access to the information they need to move forward with FarmBot.
